About me

Short bio:

Howdy! My name is Olivia Ornelas and I am the loudest and the proudest member of Texas A&M’s class of 2022. Born and Raised in San Antonio, Texas, I decided to Major in Computer Science with a minor in Cybersecurity and mathematics at Texas A&M University as a National Hispanic Recognition Scholar. At college, I joined SWElites freshman year. This freshman organization within Texas A&M University’s Society of Women Engineers (SWE) helped develop my professional development.

In addition, I was a part of a 10-person team that planned and organized a Girls Scout event called STEMFest. This event was aimed for girls in elementary to middle school and showcased different engineering organizations performing quick experiments. Sophomore year I became more involved with SWE by joining their SWEOutreach committee. SWEOutreach aims to raise awareness about other discplines within engineering and break stigmas about STEM majors to K-12 students.

On this committee, I helped plan and demonstrate small engineering projects at a local elementary school twice a month. In addition, a team of 10 people and I planned for another event similar to STEMFest but for high school students with harder STEM related challenges. This event, called SWENext, was brand new to the Texas A&M chapter, so everyone on the team gained experience with recruiting, logistics, and planning the main schedule for this program. Unfortunately, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, this event had to be cancelled.

Other than SWE

In addition to joining a SWE committee, I began attending meetings for Texas A&M’s Cybersecurity club where I got to learn introductory concepts of Cybersecurity and further break down what that major consists of. From this club I learned the history of password hacking, and ways to mitigate this issue. During spring 2020, I joined the Aggie Coding Club. This club was created as a way for people in and outside of the Computer Science major to gain experience using various coding skills and hear from different companies about their opportunities for computer science majors.

In summer 2020, I got the opportunity to intern for the Air Force’s Communication and Information division. In this program, I gained hands on experience with working under leadership and presenting research I conducted while I interned.
